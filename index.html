Phase 1

What is BODMAS?

BODMAS is the agreed order for working out expressions with more than one operation. It stops everyone from getting different answers to the same sum.

B O D M A S
BBrackets
OOrders
DDivision
MMultiplication
AAddition
SSubtraction

In this lab we focus on brackets, then × and ÷ (left to right), then + and − (left to right).

Why use it?

Without a fixed order, 2 + 3 × 4 could be read as “add first” or “multiply first.” BODMAS picks one clear path so results match textbooks, exams, and calculators.

Where it shows up

  • School maths and standardised tests
  • Science formulas (e.g. substituting values)
  • Programming and spreadsheets (similar rules)

Same expression, two stories

Take 3 + 4 × 2. If you mix up the order, the answer changes completely.

Wrong order (left to right only)
3 + 4 × 2

Do 3 + 4 first → 7

Then 7 × 2 = 14

This ignores that × is stronger than +.

Correct order (BODMAS)
3 + 4 × 2

Do 4 × 2 first → 8

Then 3 + 8 = 11

Multiplication before addition—one clear answer.

Brackets first

Whatever is inside brackets is treated as one value before the rest of the expression.

(5 + 3) × 2

5 + 3 is solved first → 8, then 8 × 2 = 16.

Rule: B — Brackets group work; simplify inside them before applying BODMAS to the outside.

Division & Multiplication

After brackets, × and ÷ come before + and −. When several × or ÷ appear, work left to right.

10 + 12 ÷ 3 × 2

First 12 ÷ 3 = 4, then 4 × 2 = 8, then 10 + 8 = 18.

Rule: D/M — Same priority band; tie-break with left-to-right order.

Addition & Subtraction

+ and − happen last. If several appear in a row, work left to right.

10 − 3 + 2

10 − 3 first → 7, then 7 + 2 = 9 (not 10 − 5).

Rule: A/S — Weakest operations; left-to-right when tied.
